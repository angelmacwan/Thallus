
# Use Case: Marketing & PR

## Problem

A marketing agency has been hired by a consumer brand to launch a new, eco-friendly product line. The goal is to generate positive buzz and associate the brand with sustainability. However, the agency is aware of the risk of "greenwashing" accusations, where consumers perceive a company's environmental claims as cynical marketing rather than genuine commitment. A failed campaign could damage the brand's reputation significantly. They need to find the most authentic way to get their message to the right audience.

## Thallus to the Rescue

The agency uses Thallus to test different influencer marketing strategies.

1.  **Seed Documents:** The simulation is seeded with the product's press kit, articles about sustainability, and posts from various online communities (e.g., zero-waste forums, ethical consumer blogs).

2.  **Agent Synthesis:** Thallus generates a population of consumer agents with different profiles: eco-conscious shoppers, brand loyalists, cynical consumers sensitive to corporate messaging, and various types of social media influencers (e.g., mega-influencers with millions of followers vs. micro-influencers with small, dedicated communities).

3.  **Strategy Simulation:** The agency runs several simulations, each testing a different strategy:
    *   **Strategy A:** A mega-influencer is paid to promote the product.
    *   **Strategy B:** The product is gifted to 20 different micro-influencers in the sustainability space, with no obligation to post.
    *   **Strategy C:** A press release is sent to traditional media outlets.

4.  **Campaign Analysis via Q&A:** The marketing team queries the simulation results to see which strategy worked best:
    *   "Compare the reach and sentiment of Strategy A vs. Strategy B."
    *   "In Strategy A, what percentage of comments accused the brand of 'greenwashing'?"
    *   "In Strategy B, which micro-influencers generated the most authentic engagement?"
    *   "What was the final purchase intent for the product in each of the three simulations?"

## Outcome

The simulation data allows the agency to advise their client with confidence. The results might show that while the mega-influencer (Strategy A) generated massive initial reach, the engagement was shallow, and a significant backlash about greenwashing occurred. Conversely, the micro-influencer campaign (Strategy B) generated less overall reach but resulted in much higher positive sentiment, authentic conversations, and a stronger purchase intent among the target demographic. The agency can now build a campaign around the strategy most likely to succeed, maximizing ROI and protecting the brand from reputational damage.
