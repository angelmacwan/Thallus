
# Use Case: Students

## Problem

A political science undergraduate is writing a thesis on the 2016 US election and wants to explore the "filter bubble" phenomenon. She has a theory that the exposure to a small amount of counter-attitudinal information would have had a negligible effect on changing opinions due to the highly polarized nature of the online environment at the time. Simply reading and citing existing papers is not enough; she wants to generate some novel data to support her argument.

## Thallus to the Rescue

The student uses Thallus, running on her personal laptop, to conduct her own small-scale, focused simulation.

1.  **Seed Documents:** She collects a curated set of 10-15 influential articles and blog posts from 2016, half strongly supporting one candidate and half strongly supporting the other.

2.  **Simulation & Intervention:**
    *   She runs a baseline simulation to establish how information circulates within two isolated, highly partisan agent clusters.
    *   She then designs a new experiment. She runs the simulation again, but after a few rounds, she manually injects one of the "pro-candidate A" articles into the feed of a few agents in the "pro-candidate B" cluster.

3.  **Data Analysis with Q&A:** The student, acting as a researcher, uses the Thallus report agent to analyze her experiment.
    *   "In the intervention run, did any of the targeted agents share the counter-attitudinal article?"
    *   "What was the sentiment of the replies to the injected article?"
    *   "Compare the belief strength of the targeted agents before and after the intervention."
    *   "Generate a network diagram showing the interactions. Did the intervention create any new cross-cluster connections?"

## Outcome

The student can include a chapter in her thesis titled "In-Silico Experiment: Modeling the Resilience of Filter Bubbles." She has moved beyond simply summarizing others' work and has created her own primary evidence, however small in scale. The simulation data and the Q&A log from Thallus provide a concrete, reproducible foundation for her argument. This elevates the quality of her thesis from a simple literature review to a piece of original research, demonstrating sophisticated analytical skills and a deeper understanding of the subject matter. It likely earns her a higher grade and provides a compelling project to discuss in graduate school applications.
