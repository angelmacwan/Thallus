
# Use Case: Consulting Firms

## Problem

A top-tier management consulting firm is advising a client in the energy sector on a major strategic shift towards renewable energy. This shift is likely to be met with resistance from various stakeholder groups, including employees in traditional sectors, local communities concerned about land use, and political commentators who are skeptical of green energy. The client needs to understand the potential landscape of public opinion and develop a proactive communication strategy to manage its reputation.

## Thallus to the Rescue

The consulting team uses Thallus to map out potential social risks and test mitigation strategies.

1.  **Seed Documents:** The consultants gather a wide range of documents representing the different viewpoints: the client's draft press releases, reports from environmental NGOs, articles from local newspapers in affected regions, and opinion pieces from hostile political commentators.

2.  **Agent Synthesis & Simulation:** Thallus creates a complex ecosystem of AI agents: concerned citizens, company employees (both for and against the shift), environmental activists, skeptical journalists, and local politicians. The simulation models how the announcement of the strategic shift ripples through this ecosystem.

3.  **Risk Analysis via Q&A:** The consultants use the Thallus report agent to identify key risks:
    *   "What are the top three negative narratives that emerged about our client?"
    *   "Which stakeholder group was most successful in spreading misinformation?"
    *   "Identify the agents who acted as bridges between disconnected protest groups."
    *   "Model the potential impact of a targeted community outreach program in 'Region X'." (By adding new seed documents and re-running the simulation).

## Outcome

The consulting firm can provide its client with a highly detailed and actionable "reputation risk report." Instead of a generic warning about "public backlash," they can deliver specific, data-backed insights: "Our simulation shows a 70% probability that a narrative around 'job losses' will be the dominant negative story, primarily amplified by local media in towns X and Y. Furthermore, the simulation indicates that a proactive community benefits agreement, announced within 48 hours, can reduce the spread of this narrative by 50%." This level of specific, predictive analysis allows the client to navigate the complex social landscape, mitigate risks, and ultimately execute their strategic shift more effectively, justifying the high fees charged by the consulting firm.
