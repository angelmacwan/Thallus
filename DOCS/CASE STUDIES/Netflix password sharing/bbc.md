## BBC News

**BBC News** is the news-gathering and broadcasting division of the British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC). It operates one of the world’s largest and most trusted news services, providing multimedia coverage across television, radio, and digital platforms. BBC News plays a central role in public broadcasting in the United Kingdom and has global reach through international bureaus.

### Key facts

- **Founded:** 1922
- **Headquarters:** Broadcasting House, London, United Kingdom
- **Parent organization:** BBC
- **Global bureaus:** 60+ worldwide
- **Languages:** English (plus more than 40 in international services)

### Origins and Development

BBC News began radio bulletins in 1922 and launched its first television news program in 1954. Over the decades, it has evolved into a multi-platform network with extensive international reporting capacity. The service was created to deliver impartial news under the BBC’s public-service charter, emphasizing accuracy and fairness.

### Structure and Operations

The division includes domestic outlets such as BBC News at Six, BBC Breakfast, and Newsnight, as well as global channels like BBC World News and the BBC World Service. It employs thousands of journalists and maintains correspondents across continents, allowing comprehensive coverage of politics, economics, science, and culture.

### Editorial Standards and Governance

Editorial decisions are guided by the BBC’s Royal Charter and the Ofcom Broadcasting Code. BBC News is legally required to remain impartial and is publicly funded through the UK television license fee. Its editorial independence is overseen by the BBC Board, which ensures adherence to its public-service mission.

### Global Influence

BBC News is regarded as a leading international news source, with its online platform reaching hundreds of millions of visitors monthly. It has been influential in shaping global journalism standards, particularly in accuracy verification, ethical reporting, and multimedia storytelling.
